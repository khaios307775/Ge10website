   With over a century since its founding, it is no surprise that Silliman University has a rich culture and along with it, traditions and events mainly held in the Claire Isabel McGill Luce Auditorium. Silliman University's culture also includes the Silliman University Culture and Arts Council (CAC), the Anthropology Museum, and the Mariano C. Lao Global Studies Center.

Paglayag 2024: Freshie walk



Silliman University Student Government (SUSG)


August 15, 2024

Role: Participant



  As a traditional event held for all freshmen going to silliman, getting cooked under the heat of the sun for an hour straight was not what I had in mind but then again, as far as tradition goes, it must be memorable. In this event, my only motivation to stay was the free ice cream at the end, so it wasn't all too bad.


  Because of this event, I had properly learned the smell of slow roasted humans. This event allowed me to meet other people and even learn all the different college courses that silliman offers. However, I’ll definitely remember this event due to the ice cream at the end due to the fact that me and my friends had a taste of all the flavors available apart from the mango flavor which we couldn’t get due to us getting full from eating ice creams.

Hibalag 2024:
Parade of Lights



Silliman University Student Government (SUSG)


August 19, 2024

Role: Spectator



  This event was held immediately after the CCS Esports event but I made it in time to watch the parade until it reached the end near the gym where the acquaintance party was being held. The parade consisted of almost all the departments of Silliman but no College of computer studies. Though there were some CCS in DOST Scholars. The parade line was quite long despite some departments not being there, a lot had flashy setups and gimmicks and one person from the psychology department even wore a clown costume which definitely gave some a traumatic repercussion


  Because of this event, I had learned that there were no torches or fire involved and simply was just a parade. Though one can argue flashlight from phones are considered torches, I beg to differ as all my life these were called flashlights or lights, although there’s no denying that regular torches are a hazard to the community and therefore, should be avoided, it was still definitely a shock not to see any fire around.

Kuwerdas Filipinas



Silliman University Culture and Arts Council


September 14, 2024

Role: Spectator



  One of the events for our Ge6, Kuwerdas Filipinas is the first semi-professional symphonic rondalla featuring 50 members in the concert tour. Immediately after entering the Luce auditorium, I noticed the abundance of the instrument bandurria. Almost half the orchestra were musicians playing this 16 stringed instrument, and it is understandable as the orchestra was Filipino themed. The rondalla showcased 13 songs with 2 additional songs after the show with 15 songs total.


  The rondalla itself was like a 3 course meal, with the first 6 songs being the appetizer, followed by the main course featuring the voice of the soprano, Stefanie Quintin, and the last 3 songs being the dessert with 2 additional songs being the cherry on top. The rondalla went on even through a storm, some were hesitant to go due to the storm and I was one of them. However, the show itself was a treat. My favorite song from the show would probably be the final song “Tropiko”, although this was not included in the main songs.
